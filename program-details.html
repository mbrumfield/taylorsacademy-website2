Are you ready for #TAE2016?

Program Structure - What is TAE?

Information

The Taylor's Academy of Excellence 2016 summer program is an eye opening two weeks for the future leaders of America from grades six to eight. The program ensures academic, professional, and personal growth for all participating leaders. The students will explore contemporary curriculum with courses that range from Discovering Identity: African American History to Mathematics. The leaders will also immerse themselves in life changing workshops where they must confront relevant issues such as teen pregnancy and gang violence. TAE also partners with local community leaders for Mentor Mania! This community cultivated mentor program gives community leaders the opportunity to speak to students every other day to prepare them for career success and future obstacles they may face.

Location

#TAE2016 will be held from June 6-19, 2016 in Ruth, Mississippi. Thanks to our gracious hosts, the Mount Zion M. B. Church Community Center will be the home to our summer learning initiatives. Transportation for the students to get to and from the location site is provided!

Final Performance

The final day of the program is a public performance of best moments of the program. Parents, community members, and teachers alike are welcome to join in celebrating history through song, dance, and skit.

Field Trip

The program also features a two day field trip to Jackson and Philadelphia, Mississippi. For leisure, the students will enjoy college tours of thee Historic Tougaloo College and Jackson State University. These young leaders will explore historic sites such as Medgar Evers home, the site were Civil Rights workers Goodman, Schwerner, and Chaney were found, and the Chahta Immi Cultural Center. Summing up the weekend, these students will also spend a half day at Geyser Falls located in Philadelphia, Mississippi.

Features - What does TAE offer?.

Interactive Lessons

The students will experience historically engaging lessons on the African American experience while producing songs and dances on contemporary issues such as police brutality.

Interactive Workshops

A few interactive workshops include "Dropping Your Genes: Understanding the Dangers of STD's," "Oops!: A Teen Pregnancy Simulation," and "Shark Tank: Creating a Small Business."

Daily Meals

The leaders will receive two full meals a day and a take home snack. Breakfast will keep the students mind's sharp while lunch will nourish them for their studies for the remainder of the day!

Professional Portfolios

The students will produce an end project of an electronic and paper portfolio that the students will present at the program's close. The portfolio will include a, résumé, one piece of writing on an historic topic of choice, a personal statement detailing their growth from the program, and many more items for their professional development.
